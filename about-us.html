Thirukadaiyur Temple - A Brief History

Thirukadaiyur Temple, also known as Sri Abirami Amman Sametha Sri Amirthakadeswarar Temple, is located in Thirukadavoor (Thirukadaiyur), in the Tanjore district of Tamil Nadu, approximately 18 km away from Mayiladuthurai. This temple, dedicated to Lord Shiva, is the eighth Veeratanam (avataram) among the eight Veeratanams of Lord Shiva.

The ancient Tamil poets Thirunavukkarasar, Sundarar, and Thirugnanasambandar have written and praised this temple. The temple is facing west and has unique trees, Pinjalam and Vilvam, as Thala Virutcham. According to mythology, Devas and Asuras were searching for Amirtham (nectar of immortality) at Thiruparkadal. While they tried to segregate the Amirtham, they forgot to worship the First and Foremost God of Hindus, Lord Ganesh. For this reason, Lord Ganesh got angry and hid the Amirthakalasam at this temple, which was later found by the Devas and Asuras at Thiruparkadal. The Amirthakalasam later became a Sivalingam in this temple. Hence, this temple Moolavar was briefly known as AMIRTHAKADESWARAR (Amirtham + Kadam).

The temple has a separate Sannathi for Lord Ganesh, known as KALLAVINAYAKAR, because of the incident from Lord Ganesh. The Kungliyanayanar and Kariyanayanar, among the 63 Nayanmars, were said to have done Sivathondu (service to Lord Siva) here.

In the ruling period of King Saraboji, Goddess Abirami Ambal made Amavasai (Moonless Night) into Pournami (Full Moon day) for a devotee named Abirami Butter, and for this, Abirami Butter praised the Goddess Abirami Ambal’s Abirami Anthathi.

Thirukadaiyur is also the place where Lord Shiva bestowed immortality to Sri Maarkandaya and bestowed Ever Sixteen Age (16). For example, Lord Shiva himself killed the God Yaksha (Yaman) and saved his votary’s life. He also bestowed Yama with Absolution for the sake of the Goddess Bhumadevi. Here, the Lord killed Yama for the sake of his votary and is known as Lord Mirthunjayamoorthi.

People perform Shastiapthapoorthi at the beginning of 61st age of their star birthday, Bheemaradha Shanthi at the beginning of 70th age of their star birthday, and Sadabishegam at the beginning of 80th age of their star birthday. Other important functions take place here, such as Ayush-Homam for children to remove future astrological difficulties arising from Navagrahas. If anyone is suffering from a serious illness and is on the verge of their lives, Mirthunja-Homam is performed here to save their lives and relieve them of all illness.
